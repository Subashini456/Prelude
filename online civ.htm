7.Natural Hazards


A natural hazard is a natural phenomenon that might have a negative effect on humans or the environment. Natural hazard events can be classified into two broad categories:geophysical and biological. Geophysical hazards encompass geological and meteorological phenomena such as earthquakes, volcanic eruptions, wildfires, cyclonic storms, floods, droughts, avalanches and landslides. Biological hazards can refer to a diverse array of disease, infection, infestation and invasive species. continue to read....

Reference Books:

"Natural Hazards and Disaster Management: Vulnerability and Mitigation" by R B Singh
"Disaster Management and Mitigation"by Prof R B Sin
